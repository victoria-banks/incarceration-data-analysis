## Overview
This project explores incarceration and disenfranchisement trends in the United States through data analysis and visualization.

The goal is to understand how incarceration rates have changed over time and how these trends differ across racial groups. Additionally, this project examines disparities in disenfranchisement, highlighting inequalities in civic participation.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze incarceration rates from 2005–2025  
- Compare trends between Black and White populations  
- Evaluate disenfranchisement rates by race/ethnicity  
- Visualize disparities using clear, data-driven graphs  

---

## Key Questions
- How has the Black incarceration rate changed compared to the White incarceration rate over time?  
- How does disenfranchisement for Black individuals compare to other racial groups?  

---

## Results & Insights

### Incarceration Trends
- Both Black and White incarceration rates have **declined over time**
- However, Black incarceration rates remain **significantly higher**
- The gap persists despite overall improvements

### Disenfranchisement
- African Americans have the **highest disenfranchisement rate (~5%)**
- This is **more than double** the rate of White individuals
- Indicates disparities in access to voting rights
